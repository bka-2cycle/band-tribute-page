A Rock Band Like No Other

I think I was in 5th grade when I heard my first Rush song. I was instantly hooked. By then the band already had a half dozen studio albums in their portfolio. Which gave me plenty of music to explore, going back to their debut album and moving forward, it was interesting to hear the music as they matured as musicians from one album to the next. The one thing that intrigued me about Rush is their song lyrics, everything from sci-fi to the trials of adolescence, but rarely touching on love or relationships, which was unique because that has always been the norm in popular Rock song writing.

A Canadian band comprised of three members. Geddy Lee, the lead singer, bass player and keyboardist, Neil Peart who played drums and wrote the majority of the bands song lyrics and Alex Lifeson on guitar. When you listen to a Rush song you notice things that you normally would not notice with rock songs of the era. Most songs move you as a whole, one melodic vessel taking you on a journey, you dont hear the parts moving, just the engine running. Rush songs have a way of making you notice the moving parts, as each band member contributes uniquely to the running engine. The melodic journey is the same as any other great song, but the individual instruments beg your attention, so much so that you eagerly anticipate drum, bass and guitar parts throughout. Each instrument taking the spotlight in a coordinated ballet of melody and rythem. To put it more sisinctly, the music just moves your senses, like no other band.

A few fun-facts about the song YYZ:

        • YYZ is the IATA airport identification code of Toronto Pearson International Airport, near Rush's hometown.

        • The song was nominated and was the runner up for the Best Rock Instrumental award in the 1982 Grammys. It lost to The Police's "Behind my Camel.

        • The crashing noise you hear between the breaks of the guitar solo is the sound of windchimes tied to a 2x4 slapped against a wood table.

        • This was one of the few songs used on the PS2 game Guitar Hero 2 that was not a remake by Wavegroup.

        • Geddy Lee's bass performance on this song has been praised by many websites and magazines as one of the best in rock history.
